Example Outreach Prompts

Guest Post Pitch Example

Create a highly personalized guest post pitch email to the editor of [Target Blog], a leading blog in the sustainable living niche. I run a website focused on zero-waste cooking and want to contribute a comprehensive guide on reducing food waste in home kitchens.

The pitch should include:

  • A personalized introduction referencing specific content on their blog that I've read and appreciated
  • A clear value proposition explaining why their audience would benefit from my content
  • 3-5 potential headline ideas for the guest post
  • A brief outline of what the article would cover
  • My relevant credentials and experience in sustainable cooking
  • Previous guest posts or publications I've contributed to (if applicable)
  • A clear, non-pushy call to action

The tone should be professional yet conversational, and the email should be structured for easy scanning. Include subject line options that would maximize open rates for this type of pitch.

Resource Link Request Example

Create a resource link request email to the website owner of [Target Website], which has a page listing resources for small business accounting. I've created a comprehensive, free calculator tool that helps small businesses estimate their quarterly tax payments.

The outreach email should include:

  • A brief, friendly introduction that shows I'm familiar with their website
  • Specific mention of their resource page (URL: example.com/small-business-resources)
  • A concise description of my tax calculator tool and what makes it valuable
  • 3-4 bullet points highlighting the key features and benefits of the tool
  • Why their audience would find this tool helpful
  • The exact URL they should link to
  • A suggestion for the anchor text they might use
  • A polite, no-pressure call to action

The email should be brief (under 200 words), genuinely helpful in tone, and avoid any language that sounds transactional or spammy. Include 2-3 subject line options that would encourage opens without appearing like a link request.

Broken Link Building Example

Create a broken link outreach email to the content manager of [Target Website], a leading resource in the digital marketing education space. I've identified a broken link on their "SEO Resources" page (URL: example.com/seo-resources) that previously pointed to a guide about keyword research tools.

I have a comprehensive, up-to-date guide on my website that would be a perfect replacement.

The email should include:

  • A helpful, non-accusatory notification about the broken link
  • The exact location of the broken link (page URL and anchor text)
  • A brief introduction to my alternative resource
  • Why my content would be valuable to their readers
  • The specific URL they could use to replace the broken link
  • An offer to help find other broken links on their site
  • A polite, low-pressure call to action

The email should position this as a win-win situation where I'm helping them improve their user experience while also offering valuable content to their readers. Include subject line options that emphasize the helpful nature of the email rather than the link request.

Digital PR Pitch Example

Create a digital PR pitch email to journalists covering technology trends. My company has just completed original research on remote work productivity trends, with surprising findings about the impact of different communication tools on team performance.

The pitch should include:

  • A compelling, news-worthy subject line
  • A concise, attention-grabbing opening paragraph highlighting the most surprising finding
  • 3-4 key statistics or findings from our research
  • Why this research is timely and relevant to current trends
  • How this data contradicts or confirms common assumptions
  • Available assets (full report, infographics, expert interviews)
  • A quote from our company's research director
  • Clear next steps if they're interested in covering the story

The pitch should be under 300 words, formatted for easy scanning, and focused on the news value rather than promoting our company. Include exclusive angles that could be offered to high-priority publications.

Expert Roundup Participation Example

Create an outreach email responding to a request to participate in an expert roundup article about "The Future of E-commerce in 2025." I am the founder of an e-commerce analytics platform with 10+ years of experience in the industry.

The email should include:

  • A thank you for the invitation to contribute
  • My 250-300 word response to the roundup question, focusing on data-driven predictions about AI personalization in e-commerce
  • A brief bio highlighting my relevant expertise and company (50-60 words)
  • Links to 1-2 previous interviews or articles I've contributed to
  • A high-quality headshot (mention of attachment)
  • My website URL and social media handles
  • An offer to provide additional information if needed

The contribution should demonstrate thought leadership with specific predictions and insights rather than generic statements. The tone should be professional but conversational, and include a unique perspective that would stand out among other experts.
